Jay Ellis Talks Nintendo, Fashion & Star Power

To help you power up your closet with our latest limited‐edition gear, we asked old‐school NES fan and current star of the HBO hit Insecure, Jay Ellis, to try on a few of our super exclusives. In between shots, we asked him to share his thoughts on sneakers, Mario power‐ups and, naturally, his first stop at Bloomingdale's.

You seem like a guy who’s pretty comfortable in a suit. What do you look for when picking one out?

I love playing with different fabrics and patterns, but I always stick with a really sharp, slim fit. I go from being really classic and traditional some days to just trying something new.

You've mentioned before that sneakers are a big part of your personal style. What's your current favorite pair?

My Adidas Tubulars are probably up there, but I'm kind of all over the place. My closet's getting ridiculous because I just don't throw anything out. I'm a hoarder when it comes to shoes.

What was your favorite style from our shoot?

I love the Theory suit I'm wearing on the cover of the men's spring catalog, but the Donkey Kong shirt is also pretty epic. I kept thinking someone was going to throw a barrel at me when I was wearing it.

In one of the looks, you're wearing a suit with a sweatshirt and sneakers. Any tips for guys at home trying to dress down a suit?

Keep it simple with really easy basics like T‐shirts, sweatshirts and a great pair of clean, minimalist sneakers.

What's your first stop at Bloomingdale's?

The Denim Shop, because I live in L.A. I wear a lot of denim. Most of my jeans are rag & bone.

What's your favorite old‐school Nintendo game?

I'm from the Nintendo generation, so I think I have to say the original Super Mario Bros. You could go to anyone's house and they'd have Super Mario Bros. Beating King Koopa was like winning the Super Bowl.

Who's your go‐to character for Mario Kart?

Yoshi. He just seems fun. Like, if Yoshi called me up and was like, "Yo, I’m in L.A. this weekend," I'd be like, "Let's go out." It would be an absolute disaster, but at the same time everyone would want to be around him.

Which would you rather have: a mushroom that makes you bigger, a feather that makes you fly or a flower that gives you firepower?

The feather that makes you fly. Can you imagine just being able to stretch out and not having to worry about the legroom on a plane? And the views!

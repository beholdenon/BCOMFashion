Red Lip Tips

First Step: PREP

The key to pulling off a red lip is pairing it with even, flawless skin. Use foundation or concealer to cover up imperfections so that your lip color doesn't accentuate any redness in your complexion. Also, make sure to gently exfoliate your lips with a warm washcloth or soft toothbrush to create a smooth canvas. Then use a hydrating lip balm and wait a few minutes for it to soak in before applying your lipstick.

Choose the right shade

Finding the right red is all about choosing the best shade for your skin tone. For fair complexions, look for colors with cool blue undertones, like raspberry and cherry. Those with medium and olive skin will look best in brown-based shades, such as brick red. For darker skin tones, try bright orange- and violet-based reds for a bold pop of color.

Make it Last

For color with staying power, start by completely filling in your lips with a long-wear liner. This makes your lips look fuller and will prevent the color from bleeding. Next, apply your first coat of lipstick, setting the color by placing a tissue over your lips and dusting a fine layer of translucent powder on top of the tissue. Complete the look by adding a second coat of lipstick, followed by gloss for a shiny finish.

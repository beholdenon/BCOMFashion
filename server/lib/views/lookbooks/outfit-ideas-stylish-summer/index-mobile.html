Entrepreneur, beauty/fitness guru and fashion’s go-to DJ Hannah Bronfman knows how to pump out a playlist of the ultimate beats—and festival looks.

How do you dress differently for a festival than you do in your everyday life?

My everyday life involves going from the gym to work to fancy DJ gigs, so I like my festival style to be more casual and edgy.

What would we find in your suitcase?

I always bring a bikini—I like to get in a few laps for a workout when I can—and a statement suit in bright colors or a print is also ideal for pool parties. Grecian sandals and mini dresses for sure. Jean shorts—the secret is that they shouldn’t be too tight or too destroyed—a leather jacket and, of course, lots of chokers. A crossbody bag is also essential because your arms need to be up in the air! Seriously, no one wants to hold anything in their hands at a festival except a drink!

Any tricks to layering for unpredictable weather?

Dressing for different weather conditions often means always having something tied around your waist for later. You also need to wear shoes that you don't mind getting dirty, like boots, if it's going to rain and get muddy.

Since you dance when you play, what looks do you find to be the most dance friendly?

Short, flowy dresses are easy and comfortable. Crop tops and skirts allow for movement. Cutoffs are hard because denim sticks to a sweaty body, but if you’re fine with those shorts riding up, then rock it!

Any signature styling suggestions that add extra attitude to any outfit?

Nail art is a must, and colored eyeliner and temporary tats are definitely a big hit. A hat is a great accessory that protects your face from the sun, and they’re also a cute way to cover up hair in humidity.

What do you think makes the festival circuit such a fun place to take fashion risks?

Music and fashion are both excellent ways to express yourself. They’ve always inspired each other. Music puts feelings into words and fashion can put those feelings into visuals.
